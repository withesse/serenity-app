# Unwind the Spiral
# 松解紧张

## Brief

An evening decompression for a body and mind that have stayed clenched for too long. It arrives at weight first, then locates where the strain is being held, repeats a gentle permission to put it down, and lets the spiral continue without asking the listener to answer it. The voice should be soft, trailing, and plain.

- Output length target: 15:00 ±45 seconds at 1× speed. Preserve the repeated refrain with identical emphasis each time.
- Delivery: soft, plain, and slightly trailing. This is release, not reassurance. Do not brighten the close.
- Pronunciations that trip stock voices:
  - EN — "spiral" stress on the first syllable; "unclenched" keep the second syllable clear; "loosen" voiced z sound
  - ZH — "松解" pronounced sōng jiě; "握着" second character neutral tone zhe; "垂下" pronounced chuí xià
- Post-processing: darker tonal balance than the daytime sessions, light room retention in pauses, and no transient enhancement.
