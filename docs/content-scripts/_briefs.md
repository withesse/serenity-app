# Session briefs — awaiting full script

Outlines for the remaining 7 sessions. Each is a compass, not a script

## deep-sleep-story — `Midnight Harbour` / `午夜港湾`

- **Duration**: 25 min
- **Voice**: Felix
- **Shape**: Long-form third-person narration. A small coastal harbour
  at midnight. Gentle movement — a boat knocking against a tyre fender,
  the tide turning, a single window lit somewhere on the hill. No
  characters, no plot — atmosphere only.
- **Arc**: 5 min orienting (the shape of the harbour, the temperature,
  the smell), 10 min sinking deeper (the water, the small animals at
  rest, the slow breath of the tide), 10 min almost nothing (space
  between sentences grows until the listener is past it)
- **Words to get right**: "tide", "creak", "salt", "unseen" / "潮", "缆绳", "咸", "看不见"
- **Avoid**: plot, named humans, any inciting event

## focus-flow — `Clear Mind Focus` / `澄明专注`

- **Duration**: 12 min
- **Voice**: Aya
- **Shape**: Not sleep. Morning/afternoon productivity warmup.
  Alert, not excited. Steady.
- **Arc**: 1 min acknowledging distractions, 3 min breath grounding,
  4 min single-point attention practice (breath → one object →
  intention), 3 min invitation to carry it into the next hour of work,
  1 min close
- **Tone**: practical, not mystical. Use "attention" not "awareness".
- **Avoid**: sleepy cadence, long silences at the end

## deep-work — `Deep Work Warmup` / `深度工作预热`

- **Duration**: 8 min
- **Voice**: Noah
- **Shape**: Even shorter, more clipped than focus-flow. Think of it
  as the 5-minute pre-game ritual before a long coding / writing
  block. Voice should be calm but alert, no pauses longer than 3s.
- **Arc**: 1 min bodily settle, 2 min intention-setting ("what's the
  one thing you're here for"), 3 min breath + visualisation of the
  work laid out in front of you, 2 min close with a subtle cue to
  open the eyes and start
- **Avoid**: goal / outcome / reward framing — the session is about
  the work itself, not what it produces

## unwind-stress — `Unwind the Spiral` / `松解紧张`

- **Duration**: 15 min
- **Voice**: Mei
- **Shape**: Evening decompression. Someone who's been clenched all
  day. The spiral is the anxiety loop that keeps retracing itself.
- **Arc**: 2 min arriving (heavy body, the day's weight), 4 min
  scanning where the tension actually lives (jaw, shoulders,
  breath shape), 5 min a repeated "I don't need to hold this right
  now" breath, 3 min letting the spiral unpack itself without being
  answered, 1 min close
- **Words to get right**: "held", "unhold", "loosen" / "握着", "松开",
  "垂下"
- **Avoid**: "problem solving" language. Nothing is being fixed, only
  put down.

## morning-light — `First Light` / `初光`

- **Duration**: 7 min
- **Voice**: Aya
- **Shape**: Waking up. Short. Bright without being loud.
- **Arc**: 1 min arriving in the body (awareness of the bed, the
  light), 2 min slow stretch + breath, 3 min one short intention for
  the day ("just this hour, what matters"), 1 min stand up / open
- **Words to get right**: "awake", "daylight" / "醒", "天亮"
- **Avoid**: coffee-ad energy. This is the minute before getting up,
  not the pep talk.

## rain-under-eaves — `Rain Under Eaves` / `屋檐下的雨`

- **Duration**: 60 min
- **Voice**: Soundscape (no narration)
- **Shape**: Pure soundscape. No script.
- **Production**: source a CC0 rain-on-tin-roof loop from Freesound
  (see `assets/licenses/soundscapes.md`), 60 minutes with a seamless
  loop point at ~59:58, stereo, −18 LUFS
- **Avoid**: thunder strikes (rules out panicked wake-ups), birds (rules
  out dawn chorus artefacts mid-session)
- Script file exists only to hold license + production notes — no
  Script EN / ZH sections needed

## forest-at-dusk — `Forest at Dusk` / `暮色森林`

- **Duration**: 60 min
- **Voice**: Soundscape (no narration)
- **Shape**: Same as rain-under-eaves — pure field recording.
- **Production**: crickets, one distant owl (spaced ≥3 min apart), wind
  in leaves. No close-mic'd insects (too startling). Dusk
  specifically — no daytime bird song.
- Same "no script body" rule.
