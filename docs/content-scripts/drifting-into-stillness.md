# Drifting into Stillness
# 缓缓入静

## Brief

A sleep-prep wind-down. Opens with body-level relaxation, moves through
slow breathing, then lets the narration thin out so the listener can
slide past it into sleep. Voice should stay soft and slightly trailing
— no warmth, no performance. Think of reading to someone who's already
half asleep.

The last minute is almost silent on purpose.

---

## Script EN

[pause:3s]

Welcome back.

[pause:2s]

Let the phone rest where it is… and let your body rest where it is.

[pause:3s]

Notice the weight of your head on the pillow.

[pause:2s]

The small sink of your shoulders as they stop holding up the day.

[pause:4s]

Take a slow breath in.

[pause:3s]

And a slower breath out.

[pause:4s]

Again. In…

[pause:3s]

…and out.

[pause:5s]

If there's any part of you still listing the day back to you — the
thing you didn't say, the thing you forgot — let it finish speaking.

[pause:2s]

You don't have to answer it tonight.

[pause:5s]

Bring your attention to your feet.

[pause:2s]

Let them be heavy.

[pause:3s]

Now your calves.

[pause:2s]

Heavy.

[pause:3s]

Your thighs. Your hips.

[pause:3s]

Let the mattress do the work of holding you.

[pause:5s]

Your chest, rising and falling on its own.

[pause:3s]

Your shoulders, soft.

[pause:3s]

Your jaw — let it be a little bit open.

[pause:3s]

Your eyes, resting behind closed lids.

[pause:5s]

Good.

[pause:3s]

The breath is still there, doing its work.

[pause:4s]

You don't have to follow it anymore.

[pause:5s]

You can let me go too.

[pause:4s]

If you hear the next few words, that's fine.

[pause:2s]

If you don't, that's also fine.

[pause:6s]

The night will carry you from here.

[pause:5s]

Goodnight.

[pause:15s]

(silence continues to fill the remaining time)

## TTS production notes

- Output length target: 10:00 ±20 seconds at 1× speed. Adjust final
  silence block if the natural read over/undershoots.
- Pronunciations that trip stock voices:
  - EN — nothing flagged
  - ZH — "列" in "列给你听" pronounced liè, not liě; "托" in "托住" first tone
- Post-processing: -16 LUFS normalisation, lowpass at 8 kHz to soften
  sibilants, 200 Hz Q 1.4 notch if the room hum shows up on the read.
